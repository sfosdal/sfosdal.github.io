For decades the Streamline has been Lower Queen Anne's living room — a dark, warm, gloriously cluttered dive where the Christmas lights never come down and everybody's welcome.

Tucked inside an unassuming old Craftsman a stone's throw from Seattle Center, it's been a neighborhood institution for years and has held down 174 Roy St since 2015. Belly up to the oval bar, rack a game of pool, feed the pinball machine, and grab a fistful of peanuts from the barrel. The walls are papered in chalk, photos, band flyers and years of regulars' handwriting. The floor is red-and-black checkerboard. The beer is cold and the pours are honest.

It's quirky, a little punk, and it feels like home — the kind of place where the TV might play an old Charles Bronson flick instead of the game, and your well-behaved dog is just as welcome as you are.
