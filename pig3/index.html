Pigs can fly, after all

Everett's classic pub, on its third life.

The original Flying Pig Food & Beverage Co. poured drinks on Colby Avenue for nearly fifteen years — a downtown institution locals never quite forgot — before closing in 2011. In 2021 the pig took flight again, landing in a turn-of-the-century brick building on Hewitt with tin ceilings, exposed brick, and a whimsical mural of pigs flying aviators' planes — a nod to Everett's aviation roots.

Now it's stretching its wings once more: expanded hours, weekend brunch, and a menu built to have a little wow factor. Same honest idea, still true. Proudly veteran-owned.

"I don't want to be everything to everyone. I want to be good at what I do — and I don't want it complicated at all."— The Flying Pig, on how they run the room
